Chess Legend Mikhail Tal

Mikhail Tal was a Soviet-Latvian chess grandmaster and the 8th World Chess Champion, known for his brilliant tactical play and fearless attacking style. He was born on November 9, 1936, in Riga, Latvia, which was then part of the Soviet Union. Tal became a grandmaster at the age of 20 and quickly rose to fame for his bold and imaginative sacrifices. In 1960, at the age of just 23, he defeated Mikhail Botvinnik to become the youngest world champion at that time. Although he lost the title back to Botvinnik in 1961, his legacy was already secure. Often called the "Magician from Riga," Tal had a unique talent for complicating positions and turning games in his favor with unexpected combinations. Despite suffering from serious health problems throughout his life, he remained a fierce competitor and one of the most beloved figures in chess. Mikhail Tal died on June 28, 1992, in Moscow, but his games and spirit continue to inspire generations of players around the world.
